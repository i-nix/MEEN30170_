Integrin Binding Energy Optimization Through Site-Directed Mutagenesis

The following outlines the computational workflow used to identify candidate amino acid residues for mutation and evaluate the resulting binding energy changes using MutateX and FoldX.

1. UniProt Sequence Information

I used UniProt to obtain the Integrin Alpha 1 Sequence.

AlphaFold Structure: View AlphaFold Model

I used AlphaFold to examine the predicted protein structure and confidence scores for the residues of interest. The ones selected had confidence scores of either "high" or "very high".

2. Secondary Structure Prediction (PSIPRED)

PSIPRED was used to predict the protein’s secondary structure, including features such as alpha helices and beta strands. The selected residues were located in coiled regions rather than disordered regions.

3. Structural Data from RCSB PDB

The protein structure file was obtained from the RCSB Protein Data Bank (PDB). This served as the input model for the subsequent interface analysis performed using PDBe PISA.

4. Interface Analysis with PDBe PISA

PDBe PISA was used to analyze the protein–protein interface. In particular, I looked at accessible surface area (ASA) and buried surface area (BSA) values to identify residues likely involved in interface interactions. Among the previously identified high-confidence residues located in coiled regions, I chose three with relatively high ASA and low BSA values as candidates for mutation.

The workflow using MutateX and FoldX for the site-directed mutagenesis is outlined below, followed by the resulting predicted changes in binding energy for one NMR model of the protein.

7. Check Chains and Residue Numbering
